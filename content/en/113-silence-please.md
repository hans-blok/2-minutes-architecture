# Silence please

**Date:** 2024-12-19 12:34

Do you recognize this? You are in a meeting. The discussion is complex, but you can sense everything: we are very close to finding a solution. And then it happens. Someone honks a question that comes completely out of the blue, and suddenly the discussion takes a completely different turn. At such a moment I often think: 'Did that person listen? Or does he just like to stir things up?' But sometimes I also wonder: is it just me? Am I insufficiently able to direct the discussion?

A great example of how we can get really good answers can be seen in the video [Steve Jobs - Art of pausing](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/anP--XSAVKs "https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#inbox?projector=1"). Here Jobs shows how powerful it can be to pause, listen carefully and ask the right questions. Another recommendation is the book *Socrates on Sneakers* by Elke Wiss. Sandra Stroeve has beautifully captured the essence of this visually — [Sandra Stroeve on LinkedIn: #socratischinterview #questions #visual #visualcommunication #leadership | 159 comments](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7126835494755131392/)

I have to admit: sometimes I don't listen well enough either. Or I don't take enough time and effort to ask that one really good question that makes the difference. Perhaps that is a good resolution for 2025: listen better and ask questions that really lead to something.

How do you deal with this? Let's inspire each other to become better at listening and leading together.
